# EU AI Act — Article 4: AI literacy

> "Providers and deployers of AI systems shall take measures to ensure, to their best extent, a
> sufficient level of AI literacy of their staff and other persons dealing with the operation and
> use of AI systems on their behalf, taking into account their technical knowledge, experience,
> education and training and the context the AI systems are to be used in, and the persons or
> groups of persons on whom the AI systems are to be used."

## What it means in practice

- **Applies to every company that deploys any AI system** — regardless of risk tier. A company
  whose entire stack is minimal-risk still has this obligation.
- **In force since 2 February 2025.** It is not deferred to August 2026.
- It is an obligation of **means** ("to their best extent"), not a certification. There is no
  official exam. But you must be able to show you took **proportionate, role-appropriate measures.**
- Enforcement/penalty provisions phased in from 2 August 2025; supervisory authorities can ask
  what you did.

## What "sufficient AI literacy" should cover (proportionate to role)

1. **What AI is in use here** — the company's own inventory of AI surfaces (ties to the inventory).
2. **Capabilities and limits** — hallucination, bias, the fact that output can be confidently wrong.
3. **Risks specific to the role** — e.g. recruiters must understand bias in CV-screening; finance
   staff must understand limits of automated credit decisions.
4. **Human oversight duties** — when and how to review, override, or escalate AI output.
5. **Data & confidentiality** — what may/may not be pasted into an AI tool.
6. **Transparency duties** — disclosing AI use to customers/candidates where required.
7. **How to report problems** — incident/escalation path.

## Building the training plan (role × content)

Group staff by exposure, not by org chart. Typical SME roles:

| Role group | Primary AI surfaces | Literacy emphasis |
|------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Leadership / ops | All; accountable for compliance | Inventory, obligations, oversight governance, incident handling |
| HR / recruiting | CV-screening, candidate evaluation | **Bias, high-risk obligations, candidate transparency, mandatory human review** |
| Finance / underwriting | Credit/eligibility/pricing models | High-risk obligations, contestability, data representativeness |
| Customer-facing | Chatbots, support copilots | **Transparency (disclose AI), accuracy review, escalation** |
| Marketing / content | Generative tools | Synthetic-content labelling, IP/confidentiality, fact-checking |
| Engineering / data | Copilots, internal tools | Secure use, logging, evaluation, change management |
| All staff (baseline) | General copilots, email AI | Capabilities/limits, data hygiene, when to involve a human |

For each role group, produce a short brief: **what they use, the top 3 risks, their oversight
duties, and the do/don't list.** Keep each to one page — that is the deliverable.

## Output discipline for the training architect

Return a plan with: a baseline module for all staff, plus one targeted module per relevant role
group present in this company. Each module: objectives, 3–6 key points, role-specific do/don'ts,
and a suggested delivery format (30-min session, onboarding doc, etc.). Note the 2 Feb 2025
in-force date so the company knows this is already overdue, not a future task.
