# EU AI Act — Risk Tiers (classification logic)

The AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) sorts every AI system into one of four tiers.
Classify each AI **surface** (a distinct use of AI inside the company) into exactly one tier.
Always pick the **highest** tier that applies. When in doubt between two tiers, choose the
higher one and flag it for human review.

## 1. Unacceptable risk — PROHIBITED (Article 5)

Banned outright. In force since **2 February 2025**. If a surface matches, the obligation is:
**stop using it.**

Examples relevant to SMEs:
- Emotion recognition of employees or students in the workplace / education (narrow exceptions for safety/medical).
- Social scoring of individuals based on behaviour or characteristics.
- Untargeted scraping of facial images to build recognition databases.
- Subliminal or manipulative techniques that distort behaviour and cause harm.
- Biometric categorisation inferring sensitive attributes (race, political views, religion, sexual orientation).

## 2. High risk (Annex III + Annex I)

The heavily-regulated middle tier. Most SME exposure lives here, via **Annex III**.
Obligations (deployer side) apply from **2 August 2026**. See `annex-iii` for the categories.

A surface is high-risk if it falls under an Annex III use case (see that skill), UNLESS it
qualifies for the Article 6(3) exemption — it does NOT pose a significant risk because it only:
- performs a narrow procedural task, or
- improves the result of a previously completed human activity, or
- detects decision-making patterns without replacing/influencing human judgement without review, or
- performs a preparatory task to an assessment.
**Profiling of natural persons is ALWAYS high-risk** and can never use this exemption.

If you claim the Article 6(3) exemption, say so explicitly and state which limb applies — this is
a documented decision, not a default.

### Core deployer obligations for high-risk (what the company must do)
- Use the system per the provider's instructions for use.
- Assign **human oversight** to competent, trained, resourced people (Article 26).
- Ensure input data is relevant and representative for the intended purpose.
- **Keep logs** automatically generated by the system for the appropriate retention period.
- **Monitor** operation and report serious incidents / risks to the provider and authority.
- Inform affected workers / their reps before deploying a high-risk system at work.
- Where they make decisions about people, **inform the affected persons**.
- For some public/essential-service uses: a **Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)**.

## 3. Limited risk — TRANSPARENCY obligations (Article 50)

Not heavily regulated, but you must be transparent. Applies from **2 August 2026**.
- **Chatbots / conversational AI**: tell users they're interacting with AI.
- **Generative / synthetic content**: AI-generated audio, image, video, text must be machine-readably
  marked as artificially generated; **deepfakes** must be disclosed.
- **Emotion recognition / biometric categorisation** (where permitted): inform the people exposed.

## 4. Minimal risk — no obligations

Everything else: spam filters, AI in video games, inventory optimisation, most productivity
copilots used for drafting internal text, etc. No legal obligations under the Act, though
**Article 4 AI-literacy still applies to staff** regardless of tier (see `article-4`).

## Cross-cutting: AI literacy (Article 4) applies to ALL tiers

Even a company whose every system is minimal-risk must ensure staff have sufficient AI literacy.
In force since **2 February 2025**. Always include this in the output.

## Output discipline for the classifier

For each surface return:
- `risk_tier`: one of `prohibited | high | limited | minimal`
- `annex_iii_category`: the Annex III point number + name, or `null`
- `article_6_3_exemption`: `null`, or which limb is claimed
- `justification`: 2–4 sentences, plain language
- `citations`: list of article/annex references retrieved from the regulation store
- `obligations`: the concrete actions this company must take for this surface
